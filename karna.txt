Karna is a major character in the Hindu epic Mahabharata. He was a warrior, a gifted speaker, and a loyal friend of Duryodhana. He fought for the Kauravas in the Kurukshetra war against his half-brothers, the Pandavas. 
Birth: 
Karna was the son of the sun god Surya and princess Kunti.
Kunti was unmarried when she gave birth to Karna.
She abandoned him in a basket on the Ganges River to avoid social backlash.
A charioteer and poet couple named Radha and Adhiratha Nandana adopted him.
Early life:
Karna was raised in secrecy, and only a few people knew the truth about his parentage. 
He faced discrimination due to his lower caste status. 
He became a great warrior and a loyal friend of Duryodhana. 
Role in the war:
Duryodhana made Karna the king of Anga (Bihar-Bengal). 
Karna fought for the Kauravas in the Kurukshetra war. 
He was killed by Arjuna in a battle on the 16th day of the war. 
Other names: 
Karna was also known as Vasusena, Anga-Raja, Sutaputra, and Radheya.
